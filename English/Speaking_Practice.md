## 🗣️ IELTS Speaking Structure & Strategies

### Part 1: Introduction & Interview (4–5 minutes)
*Casual questions about home, work, studies, hobbies, etc.*
*   **The Golden Rule:** Do not give one-word answers. Aim for **2-4 sentences** per question.
*   **Response Structure (Direct Answer + Reason + Example/Contrast):**
    *   *Question:* Do you like listening to music?
    *   *Direct Answer (1 sentence):* "Yes, absolutely, I am a huge fan of music."
    *   *Reason/Detail (1-2 sentences):* "It helps me unwind and de-stress after a long day of studying or working. I particularly enjoy acoustic and jazz genres."
    *   *Contrast/Example (1 sentence):* "In fact, I usually listen to soft jazz tracks while reading to help me concentrate."

### Part 2: The Cue Card / Individual Long Turn (3–4 minutes)
*You are given a card with a topic and 1 minute to prepare. You must speak for 1 to 2 minutes.*
*   **The PPF (Past-Present-Future) Strategy:**
    If you run out of things to say, divide your talk chronologically.
    1.  **Past:** How did you first discover this topic/object/person? What was your initial impression?
    2.  **Present:** What is the situation now? How often do you interact with it? Why is it important to you?
    3.  **Future:** Do you plan to continue doing this, or what changes do you foresee?
*   **Use the 1-Minute Prep Time Wisely:**
    Write down keywords (adjectives, phrasal verbs, idioms) instead of full sentences. Group them by paragraph/bullet point.

### Part 3: Two-Way Discussion (4–5 minutes)
*Abstract questions connected to the topic in Part 2. Focus on global issues, trends, and society.*
*   **The OREO Structure:**
    *   **O**pinion: State your view clearly. (e.g., *"It seems to me that..."*, *"I am inclined to believe that..."*)
    *   **R**eason: Explain why you hold this view. (e.g., *"This is mainly because..."*, *"The primary reason for this is..."*)
    *   **E**xample: Give a concrete or general example. (e.g., *"For instance, in many developed countries..."*)
    *   **O**pposite/Alternative (Optional): Briefly mention the counter-argument to show depth. (e.g., *"However, some might argue that..."*)
