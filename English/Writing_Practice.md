# IELTS Writing Study Guide & Templates

Use this file to practice your writing, structure your essays, and track your progress.

---

## 📝 IELTS Task 2 Essay Structure (Academic & General)
*Total Time: 40 minutes | Word Count: 250+ words | Weight: 2/3 of writing score*

### The 4-Paragraph Essay Template

#### 1. Introduction (35–50 words)
*   **Sentence 1: The Hook/Paraphrase.** Rewrite the prompt using synonyms and different grammatical structures.
*   **Sentence 2: Thesis Statement.** State your clear opinion or stance. (e.g., *I completely agree with this view...* or *While there are benefits to X, I believe Y is more significant.*)
*   **Sentence 3: Outline Sentence.** Briefly mention the two main points you will discuss. (e.g., *This essay will analyze how X improves productivity and discuss its impact on mental health.*)

#### 2. Body Paragraph 1: First Main Point (90–100 words)
*   **Sentence 1: Topic Sentence.** Clearly state the main point of this paragraph.
*   **Sentence 2: Explanation/Elaboration.** Explain *why* and *how* this point is true in detail.
*   **Sentence 3: Example.** Provide a concrete, realistic example (avoid fake statistics; use general examples like *"For instance, in many metropolitan areas..."*).
*   **Sentence 4: Concluding/Result Sentence.** Link back to your thesis statement. (e.g., *Consequently, it is clear that...*)

#### 3. Body Paragraph 2: Second Main Point (90–100 words)
*   *Follow the same structure as Body Paragraph 1, but focusing on your second point or the opposing view.*

#### 4. Conclusion (30–40 words)
*   **Sentence 1: Restate Thesis.** Paraphrase your main stance using different words.
*   **Sentence 2: Summary of Main Points.** Summarize the two arguments from Body 1 & 2.
*   **Sentence 3: Final Thought.** Provide a future prediction or recommendation (optional but adds polish).

## 📊 IELTS Task 1 Structure (Academic)
*Total Time: 20 minutes | Word Count: 150+ words | Weight: 1/3 of writing score*

### The 4-Paragraph Report Template

1.  **Paragraph 1: Introduction (1-2 sentences)**
    *   Paraphrase the prompt (e.g., *The bar chart illustrates the percentage of... between [Year] and [Year].*)
2.  **Paragraph 2: Overview (2-3 sentences) - CRITICAL**
    *   Identify 2-3 main trends, highest/lowest points, or key changes. **Do NOT include specific numbers or data here.** (e.g., *Overall, it is clear that consumption of X increased significantly, while Y saw a dramatic decline.*)
3.  **Paragraph 3: Detail Paragraph 1 (3-4 sentences)**
    *   Describe the first group of data with specific figures, percentages, or years. Group similar data together.
4.  **Paragraph 4: Detail Paragraph 2 (3-4 sentences)**
    *   Describe the remaining data, making comparisons where relevant.

## 📈 Essay Writing Checklist & Self-Evaluation
Before finalizing any practice essay, review it against this checklist:

*   [ ] **Task Achievement:** Have I fully answered all parts of the prompt? Is my word count > 250 (Task 2) or > 150 (Task 1)?
*   [ ] **Coherence & Cohesion:** Does every paragraph have a clear topic sentence? Have I used a variety of transition words (not repeating *but*, *also*, *however* too often)?
*   [ ] **Lexical Resource:** Have I avoided simple words? (e.g., used *detrimental* instead of *bad*, *proponents* instead of *supporters*, *escalate* instead of *increase*).
*   [ ] **Grammatical Range:** Did I use a mix of simple, compound, and complex sentences? Are my tenses consistent?
