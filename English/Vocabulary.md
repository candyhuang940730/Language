## 📇 Flashcards (Markdown Table format)

| Word | Meaning | Details |
| --- | --- | --- |
| mitigate | to make something less harmful, serious, or bad | Verb - Example: Governments must act swiftly to mitigate climate risks. |
| detrimental | causing harm or damage | Adjective - Example: Too much screen time can have a detrimental effect on children's development. |
| paramount | more important than anything else; supreme | Adjective - Example: In IELTS writing, clear paragraph structure is of paramount importance. |
| essential | extremely important or necessary | Adjective - Example: Clean water is essential for sustaining human health. |
| acquire | to get or buy something | Verb - Example: Children acquire foreign languages much faster than adults. |
| frequent | happening often | Adjective - Example: Heavy rain caused frequent delays on the train lines. |
| obstacle | something that blocks your way or makes progress difficult | Noun - Example: Lack of funding remains a major obstacle to the research project. |
| enhance | to improve the quality, amount, or strength of something | Verb - Example: Regular exercise can significantly enhance your immune system. |
| reliable | able to be trusted to work well and consistently | Adjective - Example: We need to find a reliable supplier for these electronic components. |
