## 📇 Flashcards (Markdown Table format)

| Grammar	 | Meaning | Details |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Not only... but also... (Inversion) | Used to emphasize two related points, with the first clause inverted. | Structure: Not only + auxiliary verb + subject + verb + but also... <br> Example: Not only did the government invest in green energy, but they also subsidized public transport. |
| Had I known... (3rd Conditional) | Used to talk about hypothetical past situations and their outcomes. | Structure: Had + subject + past participle, subject + would have + past participle <br> Example: Had the city council acted sooner, the flood damage would have been minimized. |
| It is widely believed that... | An academic passive construction used to report general beliefs objectively. | Structure: It + passive verb (is believed/argued/claimed) + that-clause <br> Example: It is widely believed that education is the most effective tool to combat inequality. |
| It is imperative that... | A subjunctive structure used to express urgency or extreme importance. | Structure: It is imperative/vital/essential + that + subject + base verb <br> Example: It is imperative that governments take immediate measures to reduce emissions. |
| Having completed... (Participle Clause) | Used to show that one action was completed before another began. | Structure: Having + past participle, subject + verb <br> Example: Having completed the research, the scientists published their findings in a journal. |
| The more... the more... | A comparative structure used to show parallel increase or decrease. | Structure: The + comparative + subject + verb, the + comparative + subject + verb <br> Example: The more resources a country invests in education, the more prosperous its economy becomes. |
| ...at / in / which... (Preposition + Relative) | Used to combine clauses precisely, sounding more formal and academic. | Structure: Noun + preposition + which + clause <br> Example: The university has established a laboratory in which students can conduct experiments. |
| Albeit | A conjunction meaning 'although' or 'even though', used to introduce a concession. | Structure: Adjective/noun + albeit + adjective/noun <br> Example: The new policy was a step in the right direction, albeit a small one. |
| Under no circumstances... (Inversion) | Used to emphasize that something must never happen, with inversion. | Structure: Under no circumstances + auxiliary verb + subject + verb <br> Example: Under no circumstances should public safety be compromised for financial gain. |
| So / Such... that... (Emphasis) | Used to express cause and effect with strong emphasis. | Structure: Subject + verb + so + adjective + that... / Such + noun + that... <br> Example: The rise in global temperatures is so rapid that many species are facing extinction. |
