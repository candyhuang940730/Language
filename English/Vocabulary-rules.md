# IELTS Vocabulary & Cohesive Devices

Use this note to store high-scoring vocabulary, collocations, and transition phrases (cohesive devices) for your IELTS preparation.

---

## 🔗 Transition Phrases (Coherence & Cohesion)

### 1. Adding Information / Supporting Points
*   **Furthermore,** (e.g., *Furthermore, study programs abroad foster independence.*)
*   **Moreover,**
*   **In addition to this,**
*   **Not only... but also...** (e.g., *This policy not only reduces congestion but also decreases air pollution.*)

### 2. Expressing Contrast & Concession
*   **On the one hand, / On the other hand,** (Use only when comparing two opposite views)
*   **However, / Conversely, / In contrast,**
*   **Despite / In spite of** (+ Noun / -ing phrase) (e.g., *Despite the high costs, public transport usage remains low.*)
*   **Albeit** (e.g., *It was an effective solution, albeit an expensive one.*)

### 3. Explaining Causes & Effects
*   **Consequently, / Therefore, / As a result, / Thus,**
*   **This can be attributed to...** (e.g., *This shift can be attributed to the rise of remote work.*)
*   **Stem from...** (e.g., *These issues often stem from poor communication.*)
*   **Pave the way for...** (e.g., *Technological advances pave the way for educational reform.*)

### 4. Giving Examples
*   **For instance, / For example,**
*   **To illustrate,**
*   **A case in point is...** (e.g., *A case in point is the city of Copenhagen, where cycling is ubiquitous.*)

## 📚 Academic & High-Scoring Synonyms
Avoid using basic verbs and adjectives. Practice replacing them with these:

| Word | Meaning | Details |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Good | Advantageous, beneficial, positive | Example: *This policy has proved highly beneficial for the community.* |
| Bad | Detrimental, adverse, unfavorable | Example: *Sleeplessness has a detrimental effect on cognitive function.* |
| Many / A lot | A myriad of, substantial, numerous | Example: *There are a myriad of options available to modern consumers.* |
| Get worse | Deteriorate, exacerbate | Example: *Ignoring structural problems will only exacerbate the issue.* |
| Solve | Mitigate, address, resolve | Example: *Governments must act swiftly to mitigate climate risks.* |
| Important | Paramount, crucial, vital | Example: *Developing critical thinking skills is of paramount importance.* |
| Use | Utilize, employ | Example: *Schools should utilize interactive technology to engage students.* |
